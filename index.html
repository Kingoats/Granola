Introduction

1. What Is Granola?

Granola has been a classic breakfast food common in America since the late 19th century. Consisting of whole grains and/or oats, nuts, honey, dried fruit and other preserved foods, this hearty breakfast has maintained its popularity over the years as a popular breakfast food, snack, and addition to baked goods, yogurt, and desserts.

The word granola was trademarked by the famous cereal maker John Harvey Kellogg, a doctor and nutritionist behind the invention of corn flakes. "Granula" was a similar recipe developed by James Caleb Jackson in 1863, subbing oats for graham flour. Today, companies all over have gone beyond Kellogg's original recipe, and created granolas to fit any craving.

Let’s take a look at the health benefits of granola in detail below.
1. Aids In Weight Loss

One of the best parts about eating granola is that it is a light yet filling food that gives the body a healthy boost in several ways. It is low in cholesterol and sodium, two factors that contribute to chronic obesity.

Furthermore, the fiber in granola makes the body feel full by bulking up food and absorbing water. As a result, it suppresses appetite and inhibits the release of ghrelin, the hormone that causes the body to feel hungry. It can help prevent overeating, which is common in obese people and those on diets that leave them hungry at odd hours of the day.

2. Improves Blood Pressure

Granola’s ability to lower blood pressure benefits your heart health as well. It relieves strain on the cardiovascular system and reduces the risk of atherosclerosis, strokes, and other potentially fatal conditions. You should supplement the potassium content of granola with fresh fruit, which is a delicious and popular combination for breakfast or a snack on the go.

3. Lowers Cholesterol Levels

Granola’s soluble fiber is a well-known way to lower harmful LDL cholesterol and promote HDL cholesterol throughout the body. By reducing unhealthy cholesterol levels in the bloodstream, you can prevent plaque formation in your arteries and veins. This type of plaque can cause high blood pressure and strain on the cardiovascular system.

Many granola recipes include nuts like almonds and walnuts, high in omega-3 fatty acids (also known as good or HDL cholesterol). You can opt for Fit & Flex Granola which comes baked and in many flavors to kick-start your day on a healthy note.

4. Helps In Disease Prevention

Granola has low levels of vitamin C, a natural antioxidant. Vitamin C boosts the immune system, stimulates white blood cells, and can help prevent cancer from forming or spreading. However, the link that researchers are focusing on right now is manganese’s role in cancer prevention.

The high manganese content of granola shows to have crucial antioxidant properties. Antioxidants are valuable compounds that seek out free radicals, which are byproducts of cellular metabolism that mutate healthy cells into cancerous ones by altering the DNA structure. By removing free radicals with antioxidants such as manganese, you can reduce your risk of developing many dangerous conditions.

5. Boosts Gut Health

Granola contains healthy ingredients that help to keep the digestive tract in good shape. When you compare it to refined breakfast cereals, the fiber in granola helps to reduce inflammation in the gut by increasing levels of healthy gut bacteria. Thus, it improves your gut health and keeps your digestive system in check.

6. Helps Prevent Anemia

Anemia is a condition that affects millions of people worldwide, but many of them are unaware that they are afflicted. It is essentially a lack of iron in the blood required for red blood cell formation. Granola contains a significant amount of iron, which helps to alleviate many of the symptoms of anemia. Some ready-made granola is more fortified with iron supplementation versus the one made at home, like Fit & Flex Granola.

7. Helps In Skin Care

Granola is high in vitamin E, also known as alpha-tocopherol, and a daily serving can supply nearly 20% of your daily requirement. Vitamin E influences several bodily processes, including protecting your skin from premature aging, sunburn, and wrinkles. It helps strengthen the capillary walls and improves your heart health, thus increased blood flow to extremities, so hair follicles and nails can function well. Overall, the copious amounts of vitamin E in granola alone make it the perfect choice for a healthy breakfast.

Recipe Using Oats

01.

Eat granola as a snack (snack)

Like other nuts, granola is eaten as a snack or snack. You can take granola anywhere very conveniently, it doesn't take much time to prepare and ensures a nutritious meal.

02.

Eat whole grain granola with dried fruit

For an extra dose of vitamins and fiber, granola is not simply made up of whole grains and seeds, but also contains dried fruit such as raisins, dried blueberries or dried berries. In other words, if you eat granola, you can add many other types of dried fruit according to your preferences.

03.

Eat granola with smoothies or fresh fruit salad

To make granola more appealing, you can add about 3 tablespoons of granola to a smoothie or fruit salad before enjoying.

04.

Eat granola with yogurt or unsweetened milk

Combining it with yogurt or unsweetened fresh milk will help you add more protein when eating granola. Not only that, the fatty taste of fresh milk or the sour taste of yogurt will make you feel delicious as well as add many other nutrients.

05.

Sprinkle granola on top of whipped cream or yogurt

Instead of mixing directly with yogurt, you can sprinkle granola on top of whipped cream and yogurt to add more appeal to the dish.

06.

Making granola hot cereal

Turning granola into hot cereal is really not too difficult. You just need to boil about 240ml of milk and 45g of granola, then stir well and cook until the mixture has a nice color, then turn off the heat. So you can enjoy it!
